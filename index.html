K9 CASE LAW: UNITED STATES & VIRGINIA

OFFICER SENSES (PROBABLE CAUSE)SIGHT, HEARING, SMELL, TOUCH, TASTE. Observations made through these senses form the basis of reasonable suspicion and PC.

FEDERAL (UNITED STATES)

1. FL v. HarrisK9 training records and certifications are sufficient to establish reliability for Probable Cause.
2. FL v. JardinesA K9 sniff on the front porch (curtilage) of a home is a search and requires a warrant.
3. Rodriguez v. USPolice cannot extend a traffic stop to wait for a K9 unit without independent reasonable suspicion.
4. Graham v. ConnorK9 use of force must be "objectively reasonable" based on the totality of circumstances.
5. US v. PlaceA K9 sniff of luggage in a public place is not a "search" under the 4th Amendment.
6. IL v. CaballesA K9 sniff during a lawful traffic stop does not require additional suspicion if it doesn't prolong the stop.
7. US v. ThomasSniffing outside an apartment door may require a higher level of suspicion in some circuits.
8. US v. SharpDetermines the reasonable length of a detention during a search.
9. Terry v. OhioBasis for "Stop and Frisk" and brief detentions for investigation.
10. Tennessee v. GarnerLimits on use of deadly force; applies to high-level K9 force.

STATE OF VIRGINIA

11. VA Code § 18.2-144.1Class 6 Felony to kill or maliciously wound a law enforcement dog.
12. VA Code § 19.2-83.1Requirements for reporting use of force, including K9 bites.
13. VA v. BrownCase law regarding K9 alerts as sufficient PC for vehicle searches in VA.
14. Consent SearchesVA standards for voluntary consent during K9 deployment.
15. Hot Pursuit ExceptionK9 tracking into private property during an active chase.
16. VA § 3.2-6500Animal Welfare standards for working LEO dogs.
17. Exigent CircumstancesSearching without a warrant to prevent destruction of evidence.
18. Plain View DoctrineSeizing evidence found by K9 handler while in a lawful position.
19. Open Fields DoctrineSearching property outside the immediate home curtilage.
20. VA Use of Force PolicyState-specific standards for K9 release and apprehension.

K9 FIRST AID & CPR

1. K9 CPR (PRIORITY ONE)100-120 compressions per minute. Lay dog on RIGHT side. 30 pumps followed by 2 nose-breaths.
2. GUNSHOT / STABPack with gauze. Apply direct pressure. Do not remove soaked pads.
3. HEAT EXHAUSTIONCool paws and ears. Move to AC. Temp >106 is fatal.
