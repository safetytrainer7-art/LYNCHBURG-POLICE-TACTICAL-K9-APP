1. Live Asset Placement & Safety

This system allows the Command Post to place and remove assets in the search area with a single tap. For the first time, every officer on the perimeter and every handler in the woods can see the live movement of their brothers and sisters in real-time. This eliminates "unknown movement" in the brush, ensuring that if you see a Blue Dot, you know it is a friendly officer, and if you see movement without a dot, you are looking at a potential threat or subject.

The distance calculator constantly monitors the gap between searching units. If units drift too far apart—creating a gap in the search line—the system will provide a haptic alert. This ensures a thorough, scientific saturation of the area, leaving no stone unturned in the pursuit of a suspect or the recovery of a missing person.

2. Perimeter Lockdown & Command Control

The Command Post view provides a high-level visual of the entire perimeter lockdown. Incident Commanders can draw a "Tactical Circle" around the last known point (LKP). This digital perimeter is visible to all units, providing a clear boundary for the search. Units can be added to the map at specific ingress points and removed once they have cleared their assigned sector, keeping the screen clean and focused on active threats.

3. Evidence & Found Property Flagging

When an officer locates an item of interest—such as a discarded jacket, a weapon, or a footprint—they can drop a "Property Flag" (Yellow Triangle) at their exact GPS coordinate. This flag is instantly visible to the entire search team. This allows the K9 handler to redirect the dog to the specific scent article without the officer having to shout or break radio silence, preserving the integrity of the crime scene and the scent cone.

4. Environmental Scent Cone Analysis

A floating wind meter and direction arrow move with the officer's position on the screen. For a K9 handler, wind direction is life. This tool allows the handler to see exactly how the wind is cutting across the terrain, indicating where a "scent cone" is likely to be blowing. By staying downwind of the Purple Dot (Subject), the handler ensures the K9 has the best opportunity for a high-intensity alert before the subject realizes they have been located.
