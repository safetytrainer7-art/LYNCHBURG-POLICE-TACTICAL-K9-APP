VA CODE § 18.2-144.1

Killing or injuring police animals; penalty. It is a Class 5 felony for any person to maliciously discharge a firearm or use a weapon to cause the death of a police animal. Class 6 felony for malicious injury with intent to maim.

FLORIDA v. HARRIS (SCOTUS)

A K9's alert provides probable cause if the dog has been certified or completed formal training. Reliability is based on the "totality of circumstances."

RODRIGUEZ v. US (SCOTUS)

A traffic stop may not be prolonged for a K9 sniff without reasonable suspicion. The search must be conducted within the time reasonably required to handle the traffic violation.

VA CODE § 18.2-460

Obstruction of Justice. Penalizes anyone who knowingly obstructs a police animal in the performance of its duties.

ILLINOIS v. CABALLES

A dog sniff conducted during a concededly lawful traffic stop that reveals no information other than the location of a substance that no individual has any right to possess does not violate the Fourth Amendment.

GUNSHOT WOUNDS (GSW)

Identify point of entry. Apply QuikClot gauze and pack the wound deep. Apply constant pressure. If on a limb, apply K9-specific tourniquet high and tight.

SNAKEBITES

Keep the K9 immobilized and the bite area below the heart. Do not use suction or ice. Identify the snake if possible (Copperhead/Timber Rattler). Transport to ER immediately.

EYE TRAUMA

Flush with saline. Use a cone or muzzle to prevent the dog from scratching. Do not apply ointments in the field.

HEAT STROKE

Move to shade. Cool paws and ears with room-temp water. Do not submerge in ice water. Temp over 106F is a life-threatening emergency.
