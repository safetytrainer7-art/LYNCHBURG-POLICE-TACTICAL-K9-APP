K9 TACTICAL MEDIC & CPR

K9 CPR: The Number One Priority

If the K9 is unresponsive and you cannot detect a pulse, you must initiate CPR immediately. Position the K9 on their right side on a firm surface. For large working breeds like Shepherds or Malinois, perform compressions at 100 to 120 beats per minute over the widest part of the rib cage. Secure the muzzle and deliver 2 steady breaths into the nostrils for every 30 compressions.

VERBAL COMMANDS: Use loud, clear verbalization for your body cam: "OFFICER DOWN—REQUESTING CODE-3 EMERGENCY VET!" and "SECURE THE AREA—MEDICAL IN PROGRESS!" Clear communication ensures dispatch and nearby units understand the severity of the situation immediately.

Penetrating Trauma & Hemorrhage

Identify all entry and exit wounds. Apply immediate pressure using hemostatic gauze. For chest wounds, an occlusive dressing (chest seal) is required to prevent lung collapse. Keep the K9 warm to fight shock, as hypothermia is a major risk in trauma cases.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "GET THE K9 TRAUMA KIT!" and "NOTIFY EMERGENCY VET—ETA 5 MINUTES!" These commands establish your professional duty of care on the record.

NARCOTICS INTERDICTION

Scent Cones & Probable Cause

Conduct vehicle sniffs from the down-wind side. Watch for the Change of Behavior (COB) such as head-snaps, tail-flagging, or "detailing" door seams. A final alert establishes the Probable Cause needed for a search under the Automobile Exception.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "AMHERST COUNTY K9—REMAIN IN THE VEHICLE!" and "K9 ALERT—PROBABLE CAUSE ESTABLISHED!" This verbal trail is essential for court testimony and body-cam evidence.

FEDERAL LEGAL SHIELD

The Fourth Amendment & K9s

Under Rodriguez v. United States, a traffic stop cannot be unconstitutionally prolonged for a K9 sniff without independent reasonable suspicion. The handler must document that the sniff was conducted during the time it took to complete the mission of the stop (writing the ticket or checking the license).

VERBAL COMMANDS: "STOP MISSION CONTINUING—DEPLOYING K9!" and "DIVERSIONARY MISSION COMPLETE!" Documenting these moments verbally helps defend against suppression motions in court.
