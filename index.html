K9 FIRST AID

1. K9 CPR

Lay dog on right side. Place hands over the widest part of the chest. Compress 1/3 to 1/2 chest width at 100-120 BPM. Allow full recoil.

Deliver 2 rescue breaths every 30 compressions by breathing into the nose with the mouth held shut. Continue until help arrives or the dog resuscitates.

2. POISON / TOXIN EXPOSURE

Identify the substance immediately. If the toxin was skin-contact, flush the area with massive amounts of lukewarm water for 20 minutes. Do not induce vomiting unless specifically directed by a vet, especially if the substance was caustic (like bleach) or petroleum-based, as it will cause more damage coming back up.

If the dog is seizing or unconscious, ensure the airway is clear and do not put anything in the mouth. Collect a sample or photo of the toxin for the vet. Every second counts; contact a pet poison control hotline or the nearest emergency vet while en route.

3. EYE INJURIES

Do not allow the dog to rub or paw at the eye, as this will quickly turn a minor scratch into a permanent rupture. If a foreign object is visible, do not attempt to remove it with tweezers. Instead, flush the eye gently with sterile saline solution to move the object toward the corner for removal.

If the eyeball is displaced from the socket (proptosis), keep the eye moist with saline and a clean cloth. Do not attempt to push it back in. This is a surgical emergency; cover the eye loosely with a damp dressing and transport immediately to save the dog's vision.

4. BROKEN BONES / FRACTURES

Check for open wounds near the fracture site; if bone is protruding, cover it with a sterile dressing but do not try to "set" or move the bone. Use a muzzle if the dog is in high pain to prevent accidental bites to the handler. Minimize all movement of the affected limb to prevent further nerve or vascular damage.

Improvise a splint only if transport will be delayed, using rolled magazines or heavy cardboard secured with medical tape—ensuring the splint extends above and below the joint. Support the dog’s weight using a sling or towel under the belly when moving them into the vehicle.
