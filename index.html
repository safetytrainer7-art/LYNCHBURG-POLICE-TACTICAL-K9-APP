VA Code § 18.2-144.1. Killing or injuring police animals; penalty.

A. Any person who (i) maliciously discharges a firearm or (ii) maliciously uses any weapon or other instrument and thereby causes the death of any animal that he knows or has reason to believe is a police animal for the purpose of aiding law-enforcement officers is guilty of a Class 5 felony.

B. Any person who maliciously kills or maliciously causes bodily injury to a police animal... is guilty of a Class 6 felony.

VA Code § 18.2-460. Obstruction of Justice.

Any person who without just cause knowingly obstructs... any law-enforcement officer or any animal being used by a law-enforcement officer... in the performance of his duties... is guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor.

Florida v. Harris (2013) - 568 U.S. 237

The Supreme Court ruled that a K9's alert provides probable cause if the animal has been trained and certified. The Court rejected a "strict checklist" for reliability, stating that if a dog has completed a certification or training program, a court can presume the dog’s alert is reliable.

US v. Place (1983) - 462 U.S. 696

The "Sniff Test" Ruling. The court held that a sniff by a well-trained narcotics detection dog does not constitute a "search" within the meaning of the Fourth Amendment because it is "so limited both in the manner in which the information is obtained and in the content of the information revealed."

Florida v. Jardines (2013) - 569 U.S. 1

The Court held that using a drug-sniffing dog on a homeowner's porch to investigate the contents of the home is a "search" and requires a warrant. The porch is considered "curtilage" and enjoys the same protection as the home itself.

K9 CPR & Resuscitation

1. Position: Place K9 on right side (left side up).

2. Compressions: Place hands over the widest part of the chest (behind elbow). Depress chest 1/3 to 1/2 of its width.

3. Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute.

4. Ratio: 30 compressions followed by 2 breaths into the snout (mouth held closed).

K9 Narcan Deployment

1. Identify: Pinpoint pupils, collapse, slow/no breathing.

2. Dose: 4mg Nasal Spray. Use one full device in one nostril.

3. Note: K9s may require higher or repeat doses due to metabolic speed. Monitor closely for 15-30 minutes.

Hyperthermia (Heat Stroke)

1. Critical Temp: Rectal temp above 106°F is a life-threatening emergency.

2. Cooling: Move to shade/AC. Apply cool (not ice) water to paws, ears, and groin.

3. Warning: Do NOT submerge in ice water (causes shivering and spikes internal temp).

Gunshot/Trauma & Tourniquet

1. Tourniquet: Apply high and tight on the limb. Use a windlass style (CAT) and tighten until bleeding stops.

2. Chest Wounds: Use an occlusive dressing (chest seal) for sucking chest wounds. Tape on 3 sides.
