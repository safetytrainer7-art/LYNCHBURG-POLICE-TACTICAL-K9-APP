K9 TRAUMA & FIRST AID

Post-Apprehension Care

Once a suspect is secured, the officer has a constitutional duty to provide medical aid for K9 bites. Failure to act can result in 'deliberate indifference' claims. Document the time aid was rendered and call EMS immediately.

LEGAL SHIELD: CASE LAW

1. Rodriguez v. United States (Federal)

The Law: A traffic stop cannot be prolonged beyond the time necessary to complete the initial mission (checking license/registration) just to wait for a K9.

Application: Conduct your K9 sniff while the second officer is writing the citation to avoid an illegal seizure.

2. Virginia v. Robertson (State)

The Law: A certified K9's alert provides full probable cause for a warrantless search of a vehicle in Virginia.

Application: Document the dog’s training and the specific 'final response' (sitting or staring) to protect the probable cause in court.
