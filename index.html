K9 TACTICAL MEDIC & CPR

K9 CPR: Primary Life Support

If the K9 is unresponsive and pulseless, begin CPR immediately. Lay the K9 on their right side. Compress the chest at 100-120 BPM over the widest part of the ribs. Deliver 2 breaths into the nostrils for every 30 compressions. Continue until the K9 regains a pulse or you reach a veterinary facility.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "OFFICER DOWN—CODE 3 VET EMERGENCY!" and "STAY BACK—MEDICAL IN PROGRESS!" These commands establish your leadership and ensure the scene is recorded as a priority medical event.

NARCOTICS INTERDICTION

Scent Cones & Probable Cause

Conduct vehicle exterior sniffs starting from the down-wind side. Identify the "Scent Cone" and watch for Change of Behavior (COB) such as head-snaps or intensive detailing. A final alert establishes Probable Cause for a search under the Automobile Exception.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "ACSO K9—REMAIN IN THE VEHICLE!" and "K9 ALERT—PROBABLE CAUSE!" These verbal cues link the dog's behavior to your tactical decision for court testimony.

FEDERAL LEGAL SHIELD

Rodriguez v. United States

The Fourth Amendment prohibits prolonging a traffic stop for a K9 sniff without independent reasonable suspicion. Documentation must show the sniff occurred during the time required to complete the "mission" of the stop, such as the records check or summons writing.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "MISSION CONTINUING—DEPLOYING K9!" and "STOP MISSION COMPLETE!" These phrases on body-cam provide a clear timeline for the prosecutor to defend the search.

K9 ADMINISTRATION

Reliability & Training Records

A K9 team's credibility is built on their training logs. Every session should be recorded to show accuracy rates and performance in various environments. These records are the first thing a defense attorney will request during a suppression hearing.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "TRAINING START!" and "REWARD GIVEN—SESSION COMPLETE!" Proper verbal cues during training ensure the dog is conditioned for the same clarity in the field.
