LEGAL BIBLE

FEDERAL STANDARDS

Fourth Amendment: Per US v. Place, a K-9 sniff is not a search because it only discloses contraband. However, Rodriguez v. US is the "clock" handler. You cannot extend a traffic stop by even one minute to wait for a dog unless you have independent Reasonable Suspicion.

Reliability: Florida v. Harris states that certification by a reputable body is enough for Probable Cause. You don't need a checklist of every success/failure, but your training logs must be current to prove the dog is "well-trained."

Deployments: Graham v. Connor applies to K-9 bites. Use of force must be "objectively reasonable." Always give a clear warning ("Sheriff's Office: Come out or I'll release the dog!") before deployment to mitigate liability.

VIRGINIA CASE LAW

Curtilage: Florida v. Jardines (upheld in VA) prevents bringing a dog onto a porch (curtilage) without a warrant. If the front door sniff is the only basis for the warrant, the evidence will be suppressed. Stay in the public-access areas.

Probable Cause: In Virginia, a final alert by a certified K-9 provides immediate PC to search every part of the vehicle, including locked containers, under the Automobile Exception. Document the dog's specific "alert" behavior clearly.

Search Warrants: When applying for a house warrant based on a dog, Virginia magistrates require proof of the dog's certification and the handler's experience. "Stale" alerts (hours after the fact) are often rejected in the Commonwealth.

DRUGS & SAFETY

ID & WEIGHTS

Fentanyl/Synthetics: The blue "M30" press is the most common killer. It mimics Oxycodone but is chalky and inconsistent. If you see powder or pills, use Nitrile gloves. Never field test; a microscopic amount of Carfentanil can be fatal.

Meth/Heroin: "Ice" meth looks like jagged clear glass. Heroin in VA is often "Grey Death" (Heroin + Fentanyl). Watch for "stamps" on wax paper or corner-cut baggies which indicate Distribution (PWID) intent.

VA Thresholds: 10 grams of Meth or 5 grams of Crack triggers major mandatory minimums. Always document scales, cash, and "pay/owe" sheets separately from the narcotics to build the PWID case for the Commonwealth's Attorney.

K9 FIRST AID

TACTICAL MEDICAL

Bloat (GDV): The stomach twists, cutting off blood. Symptoms: non-stop retching and a drum-like belly. This is a surgical emergency. Transport at high speed; keep the dog's head up and prevent them from rolling.

Narcan: 4mg Nasal Spray. If your dog is lethargic or has pinpoint pupils after a search, hit them with Narcan immediately. Large dogs may need 2-3 doses. Monitor breathing; the effect can wear off before the drug is out of the system.

Heatstroke: Temp >104°F. Cool water on paws/ears/belly. NEVER use ice water; it causes vasoconstriction and keeps the heat in the core, cooking the organs. Use a fan and get to the vet immediately.

Trauma/GSW: Apply direct pressure. Use a muzzle if the dog is conscious (pain = biting). For limb wounds, use a tourniquet high and tight. Keep the dog warm to prevent shock.
