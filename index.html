K9 FIRST AID & TRAUMA

Penetrating Trauma / Gunshot

TACTICAL CARE: Secure the scene first. Identify entry and exit wounds. Use occlusive (chest seal) dressings for torso wounds to prevent lung collapse. For limb hemorrhage, use direct pressure with hemostatic gauze. Avoid tourniquets on K9s unless the limb is unsalvageable.

Snake Bites (Pit Vipers)

VA PROTOCOL: Identify if Copperhead or Timber Rattler. Keep the K9 calm and the bite area below heart level. DO NOT cut the wound or use suction. DO NOT apply ice. Rapid transport to a facility with antivenom is the only effective treatment.

NARCOTICS INTERDICTION

Masking Agents & Indicators

DOCUMENTATION: Always document the presence of "masking odors" such as dryer sheets, mustard, coffee, or heavy air fresheners. While not PC alone, they are critical for building "Reasonable Suspicion" in your narrative for a K9 deployment.

The Search Pattern

FIELD WORK: Conduct a 360-degree exterior sniff. Focus on "Scent Pools" at the door seams and trunk seal. Document the specific Change of Behavior (COB) such as head-snaps, tail-flagging, or closed-mouth breathing prior to the final alert.
