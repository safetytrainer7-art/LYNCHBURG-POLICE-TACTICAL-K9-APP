K9 CASE LAW BIBLE

1. Florida v. Harris (2013) – K9 Reliability
The Court held that a K9’s reliability is determined by its training records and certifications. If the State provides evidence of a dog’s successful completion of a certification program, an officer has probable cause for a search following an alert.
This emphasizes that the "totality of circumstances" matters. It validates that a certified dog is a reliable tool, but places the burden on the handler to maintain meticulous logs.
2. Florida v. Jardines (2013) – Curtilage Sniffs
The Supreme Court ruled that using a drug-sniffing dog on a homeowner's porch constitutes a "search" under the Fourth Amendment. An officer cannot bring a K9 to the "curtilage" of a home for a sniff without a warrant or specific exception.
This means while a dog can sniff a car on a public road, it cannot be used at the front door of a private residence without legal cover. Handlers must stay on the public sidewalk or driveway.

K9 EMERGENCY MEDICAL

K9 CPR PROTOCOL
Lay K9 on RIGHT side. Compress 100-120 BPM over the heart. Maintain 30 compressions to 2 breaths into the nose. Continue until pulse returns or at emergency vet.
HEAT STROKE EMERGENCY
Normal temp is 101-102.5. If over 106, it is life-threatening. Move dog to shade/A/C. Use cool water (not ice) on paws and ears. Stop cooling once temp reaches 103.5. Organ damage can occur even if dog looks better.
