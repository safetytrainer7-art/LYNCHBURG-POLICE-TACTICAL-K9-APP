K9 TACTICAL MEDIC & CPR

K9 CPR: The Number One Priority

PROTOCOL: If the K9 is unresponsive with no detectable pulse, initiate CPR immediately. Place the K9 on their right side. For large dogs, compress the widest part of the rib cage; for tapered-chest dogs (like Shepherds), compress directly over the heart. Aim for 100-120 compressions per minute, allowing full chest recoil. Give 2 breaths for every 30 compressions by closing the K9's mouth and breathing directly into the nose.

VERBAL COMMANDS: While performing CPR, the handler must maintain scene leadership. Use loud, clear verbalizations: "OFFICER DOWN—REQUESTING IMMEDIATE EMS AND VETERINARY SECURE TRANSPORT!" and "STAY BACK—K9 EMERGENCY IN PROGRESS!" Clear communication prevents civilian interference and ensures the department is notified of the severity of the K9's status immediately.

Penetrating Trauma & Hemorrhage Control

FIELD TRIAGE: Once the airway is clear and the heart is beating, address "M.A.R.C.H." (Massive Hemorrhage, Airway, Respirations, Circulation, Hypothermia). For gunshot wounds, identify entry and exit points. Apply occlusive dressings to torso wounds to prevent "sucking" chest injuries that lead to lung collapse. Use hemostatic gauze for limb bleeds and apply constant, heavy pressure for at least 3-5 minutes without checking the wound.

VERBAL COMMANDS: During medical intervention, command bystanders or other deputies to assist: "SECURE THE PERIMETER—DO NOT LET ANYONE THROUGH!" and "GET THE K9 TRAUMA KIT NOW!" Constant verbalization documents your active "Duty of Care" for anyone recording the scene, proving the department acted with professional urgency and tactical precision.

NARCOTICS INTERDICTION

The Search Pattern & Change of Behavior

METHODOLOGY: Begin the vehicle exterior sniff from the down-wind side. Allow the K9 to work the "Scent Cone" naturally. Watch for the Change of Behavior (COB): a sudden head-snap, a change in tail carriage, or the K9 "detailing" a specific seam or handle with closed-mouth breathing. A final response (sit or down) establishes probable cause for a warrantless search under the Automobile Exception, provided the stop was not unconstitutionally prolonged.

VERBAL COMMANDS: Before deploying, give the loud, formal announcement: "ATTENTION—AMHERST COUNTY SHERIFF'S OFFICE K9! REMAIN IN YOUR VEHICLE OR THE K9 WILL BE DEPLOYED!" Once the K9 alerts, verbalize the finding for your body camera: "K9 ALERT—PROBABLE CAUSE ESTABLISHED!" This verbal trail is vital for the Commonwealth Attorney to win the case in court, as it links the dog's physical alert to your immediate tactical decision.

Masking Agents & Criminal Indicators

INTERDICTION: Always document the presence of masking agents like heavy perfume, dryer sheets, or coffee grounds. While these are not probable cause alone, they are critical components of "Reasonable Suspicion" in a suppression hearing. Note the suspect's behavior during the sniff; "over-compliance" or extreme nervousness combined with masking agents often indicates a high-weight transport. Documenting these environmental factors shows the court the "Totality of Circumstances."

VERBAL COMMANDS: During the vehicle search, maintain control: "STEP BACK FROM THE VEHICLE!" and "KEEP YOUR HANDS WHERE I CAN SEE THEM!" If the K9 is exposed to a masking agent or toxic substance, immediately command: "SEARCH CEASED—TOXIC EXPOSURE!" and move the K9 to clear air. This protects the asset and demonstrates your priority on K9 safety over the seizure, which looks excellent in a professional reference letter.
