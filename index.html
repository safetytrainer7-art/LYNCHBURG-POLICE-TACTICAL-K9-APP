LEGAL SHIELD BIBLE

FEDERAL CASE LAW

Rodriguez v. United States (2015)

Officers cannot prolong a traffic stop to conduct a K9 sniff without independent reasonable suspicion. The 'mission' of the stop is completed when the citation is issued or should have been issued.

Florida v. Jardines (2013)

The curtilage (porch/immediate yard) of a home is a protected area. A K9 sniff at the front door requires a warrant.

Graham v. Connor (1989)

Use of force must be 'objectively reasonable' from the perspective of an officer on the scene, not with 20/20 hindsight.

VIRGINIA STATUTES

VA Code § 18.2-144.1

Felony to maliciously wound or kill a law enforcement animal. Protects working dogs during the line of duty.

K9 EMERGENCY SOS

FIELD TRAUMA TRIAGE

HEAT STRESS: Seek shade. Cool water on pads, ears, and groin. Monitor for heavy panting or collapse.

BLOAT (GDV): Dry heaving, distended abdomen, restlessness. Transport immediately—this is a life-threatening emergency.

BLEEDING: Apply steady, direct pressure with field gauze. Do not remove dressing if it soaks through; add more on top.
