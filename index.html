HEAT EXHAUSTION / BLOAT

Heatstroke: If the dog's temp exceeds 104°F, apply cool water to the belly, paws, and ears. 100% vital: Do NOT use ice water, as it causes blood vessels to constrict and traps heat inside the core.

Bloat (GDV): Watch for unproductive retching, a "tight" drum-like stomach, and restlessness. This is a 10/10 emergency. In a tactical environment, keep the dog sternal (on chest) and transport at high priority to a vet with surgical capabilities.

Narcan: For suspected opioid exposure, use nasal Narcan (4mg). A large K-9 may require multiple doses. Ensure the dog's airway is clear and use a muzzle only if they are conscious and aggressive.
