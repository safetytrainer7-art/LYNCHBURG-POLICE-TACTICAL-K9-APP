K9 FIRST AID & TRAUMA

Gunshot Wounds / Penetrating Trauma

Immediate Action: Identify entry/exit wounds. Apply occlusive dressing to chest wounds to prevent "sucking" chest injury. Apply direct pressure to extremities.

Heat Exhaustion / Stroke

Immediate Action: Move K9 to shade. Apply cool (not ice) water to paw pads, groin, and armpits. Do not force-water if K9 is semi-conscious.

NARCOTICS DETECTION PROTOCOLS

Vehicle Exterior Sniff

Procedure: Always start down-wind. Allow K9 to detail seams and door handles. Document "Change of Behavior" (COB) vs "Final Response."

Masking Agents

Documentation: Explicitly note the presence of dryer sheets, coffee grounds, or heavy perfume. These are indicators of "Criminal Interdiction" behavior and support Reasonable Suspicion.

FEDERAL LEGAL SHIELD

Rodriguez v. United States (2015)

The "Mission" of the stop cannot be detoured for a K9 sniff. If the ticket is written, the stop is over. Run the dog during the records check.
