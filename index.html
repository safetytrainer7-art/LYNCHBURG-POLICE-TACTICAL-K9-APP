🩹 K9 FIELD MEDICAL & VETS
1. K9 CPR (IMMEDIATE)

Lay dog on RIGHT side. Locate heart at left elbow. Compressions: 100-120 per minute. 2 breaths every 30 compressions. Extend neck to open airway.

2. EMERGENCY VETS (AMHERST/LYNCHBURG)

Animal Emergency Lynchburg: (434) 846-1504 (24/7)
Laurel Stone Amherst: (434) 946-5059

3. GUNSHOT / TRAUMA

Pack wound deep with QuikClot or gauze. Apply constant pressure for 5 mins. TQ high and tight for limb wounds.

4. NARCAN DEPLOYMENT

4mg Intranasal for suspected opioid exposure (Fentanyl). Monitor breathing; repeat every 3 mins if needed.

5. HEAT STROKE

Cool paws/ears with water. Do not use ice water. Move to shade. Immediate transport if temp >106F.

6. SNAKEBITE

Keep K9 calm. Bite below heart. Transport to Lynchburg ER for antivenin. Do not cut or suck wound.
