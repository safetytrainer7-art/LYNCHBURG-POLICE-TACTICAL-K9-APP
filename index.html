FEDERAL CASE LAW

Terry v. Ohio (1968)

Authorized "Stop and Frisk" based on Reasonable Articulable Suspicion of a crime and weapon.

Carroll v. United States (1925)

The "Automobile Exception." Probable cause allows vehicle search without a warrant due to mobility.

Mapp v. Ohio (1961)

Exclusionary Rule: Evidence obtained in violation of the 4th Amendment is inadmissible.

VA STATE CODE (SELECTED)

§ 18.2-460

Obstruction of Justice: Knowingly obstructing a Law Enforcement Officer in the performance of their duties.

§ 18.2-388

Public Intoxication: Appearing intoxicated in public (Alcohol or Drugs).

§ 18.2-415

Disorderly Conduct in Public Places.

TACTICAL EMERGENCY CARE
MASSIVE HEMORRHAGE (TOURNIQUET)

1. Apply high and tight on the limb.
2. Tighten until bleeding stops.
3. Note time (T) on forehead or strap.

AIRWAY / CHEST WOUND

If sucking chest wound: Apply occlusive dressing (vented if possible) taped on three sides.

OVERDOSE RESPONSE (NARCAN)

1. Check for responsiveness.
2. Insert nozzle into nostril and spray.
3. Administer second dose after 2-3 mins if no change.

CPR (ADULT)

100-120 compressions per minute at 2" depth. Allow full chest recoil.
