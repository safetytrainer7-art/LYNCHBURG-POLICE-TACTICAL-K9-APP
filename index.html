VA Code § 18.2-144.1. Killing/Injuring Police Animals.

Any person who maliciously discharges a firearm or uses any weapon to cause the death of a police animal aiding LEO is guilty of a Class 5 felony. Malicious injury is a Class 6 felony.

VA Code § 18.2-460. Obstruction of Justice.

Knowingly obstructing any animal being used by LEO in the performance of duties is a Class 1 Misdemeanor.

Florida v. Harris (2013)

K9 reliability for Probable Cause is established by training records and certifications. Strict checklists are not required if certification is current.

US v. Place (1983)

A K9 sniff of luggage in a public place is not a search under the 4th Amendment due to its limited nature.

Florida v. Jardines (2013)

LEO cannot use a drug dog on the curtilage (porch) of a home without a warrant.

K9 CPR (Field Protocol)

1. Right side down. 2. Hands over heart (behind elbow). 3. 100-120 compressions/min. 4. 30:2 ratio (30 pumps, 2 snout breaths).

K9 Narcan (4mg)

For opioid exposure: lethargy, pinned pupils. Administer intranasally. Repeat every 3-5 mins if no response.

Heat Stroke (Hyperthermia)

Temp >106 is critical. Shade and cool water on paws, ears, groin. Transport immediately.

K9 Tourniquet

High and tight on limb. Tighten windlass until bleeding stops. Record time.
