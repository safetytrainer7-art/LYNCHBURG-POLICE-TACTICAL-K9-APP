K9 FIRST AID & TRAUMA

▶ GUNSHOT & PENETRATING TRAUMA

FIELD TRIAGE: Immediately assess for entry/exit points. For chest wounds, apply an occlusive dressing (vented if available) to prevent tension pneumothorax. Use a tourniquet for catastrophic limb hemorrhage only as a last resort, as it can cause permanent nerve damage to working dogs.

▶ NARCAN ADMINISTRATION

EXPOSURE: If a K9 exhibits pin-point pupils, respiratory depression, or ataxia following a narcotics search, administer 4mg Intranasal Naloxone (Narcan) immediately. Be prepared for the K9 to wake up in a state of agitation or aggression.

NARCOTICS INTERDICTION

▶ VEHICLE EXTERIOR SNIFF

METHODOLOGY: Begin the approach from down-wind to maximize the K9's scent cone. Watch for "Head Snaps" or slowed respiration (Closed-Mouth sniffing). A final sit or down response creates the "Automobile Exception" for a warrantless search.

▶ CURTILAGE & SNIFFS

RESTRICTION: Per Florida v. Jardines, a K9 sniff at the front door of a residence is considered a "Search" under the 4th Amendment. You MUST have a warrant or consent before bringing a dog onto a private porch for the purpose of a narcotics sniff.
