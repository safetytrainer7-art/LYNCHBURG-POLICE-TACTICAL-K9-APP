K9 TACTICAL MEDIC & CPR

K9 CPR: Life-Saving Baseline

If a K9 is non-responsive and pulseless, begin CPR immediately. Lay the K9 on their right side. For most working breeds, compressions should be delivered at the widest part of the chest at 100-120 BPM. Secure the muzzle and deliver 2 quick breaths through the nose for every 30 compressions until professional veterinary care is reached.

VERBAL COMMANDS: Maintain scene command by shouting clearly: "OFFICER DOWN—CODE 3 VETERINARY EMERGENCY!" and "ALL UNITS STAND BY—K9 MEDICAL EMERGENCY IN PROGRESS!"

NARCOTICS AIDS

Scent Cones & Alerts

Identify "Scent Pools" around vehicle seams. Watch for "Head Snaps" and changes in respiration. A final alert—typically a sit or down—provides the probable cause required for a search under the Automobile Exception.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "AMHERST COUNTY K9—REMAIN IN THE VEHICLE!" and "K9 ALERT—PROBABLE CAUSE!"

LEGAL SHIELD

Rodriguez v. United States

Under Federal Case Law, a traffic stop cannot be unconstitutionally prolonged for a K9 sniff without independent reasonable suspicion. Documentation must show the sniff occurred during the time required to complete the mission of the stop.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "TRAFFIC MISSION CONTINUING!" and "DEPLOYING K9!"

Reliability Documentation

Virginia Statutory Authority
