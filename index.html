K9 CASE LAW BIBLE (US/VA)

1-5 SENSES: Sight, Hearing, Smell, Touch, Taste. (Foundation of PC).
1. FL v. Harris: Training records/Certs = K9 reliability.
2. FL v. Jardines: No sniffs on porch without warrant.
3. Rodriguez v. US: Cannot prolong stop for K9.
4. VA Code 18.2-144.1: Felony to harm LEO K9.
5. VA v. Brown: K9 alert = Probable Cause in VA.
6. US v. Place: Airport sniff != Search.
7. IL v. Caballes: K9 sniff during lawful stop is okay.
8. Graham v. Connor: Use of force - Reasonableness.
9. TN v. Garner: Deadly force restrictions.
10. Terry v. Ohio: Reasonable suspicion for stop/frisk.
11. US v. Thomas: Apartment door sniffs (varies by circuit).
12. City of Indianapolis v. Edmond: K9 drug roadblocks unconstitutional.
13. Robinette v. Barnes: K9 as deadly force (not per se).
14. VA v. Miller: VA rules on K9 tracking reliability.
15. PA v. Muniz: Physical vs Testimonial evidence.
16. Mapp v. Ohio: Exclusionary rule.
17. US v. Sharp: Duration of stop for investigation.
18. VA Code 19.2-83.1: LEO reporting requirements.
19. Kerr v. West Palm Beach: K9 deployment standards.
20. Chew v. Gates: Excessive force in K9 bites.
