K9 TRAUMA & FIRST AID

Post-Apprehension Medical Duty

The Law: Once a suspect is secured, the 4th Amendment requires "Reasonable" care. Failure to provide aid for K9 bites can result in "Deliberate Indifference" litigation.

Documentation: Record the time of apprehension vs. the time aid was rendered. Use the phrase "Immediate medical assessment conducted" in your final report.

Field Application: Apply direct pressure to bite wounds as soon as cuffs are secure; summon EMS immediately for any arterial bleeding.

FEDERAL LEGAL SHIELD

Rodriguez v. United States (2015)

The Law: SCOTUS ruled that a traffic stop may last no longer than is necessary to address the "mission" of the stop. Prolonging it for a K9 sniff without independent suspicion is unconstitutional.

Documentation: Log the exact time the records check was completed. If the sniff occurred simultaneously with the check, the search is valid.

Field Application: Use a two-officer approach. One officer writes the summons while the handler conducts the open-air sniff to avoid "prolonging" the stop.

VIRGINIA STATE CODE

VA Code § 3.2-6500 (Fresh Pursuit)

The Law: Virginia law allows K9 entry into "curtilage" (yards/porches) during fresh pursuit of fleeing felons or endangered persons.

Documentation: Articulate the "start point" of the track and the "exigency" (public safety risk) to justify entering private property without a warrant.

Field Application: Follow the scent trail directly; if the suspect is caught on a porch, your GPS "Breadcrumb" trail proves the dog led you there.
