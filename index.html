K9 TACTICAL MEDIC & CPR

K9 CPR: Life-Saving Baseline

FIELD PROTOCOL: If a K9 is non-responsive and pulseless, begin CPR immediately. Lay the K9 on their right side. For most working breeds, compressions should be delivered at the widest part of the chest at 100-120 BPM. Secure the muzzle and deliver 2 quick breaths through the nose for every 30 compressions. This maintains oxygen flow to the brain until advanced veterinary care can be reached.

VERBAL COMMANDS: Maintain scene command by shouting clearly: "OFFICER DOWN—CODE 3 VETERINARY EMERGENCY!" and "ALL UNITS STAND BY—K9 MEDICAL EMERGENCY IN PROGRESS!" Clear verbalization ensures dispatch is aware of the priority and prevents civilian interference during the resuscitation attempt.

Penetrating Trauma & Blood Loss

FIELD ACTION: Check for entry and exit wounds from gunshots or impalements. Use hemostatic gauze for limb bleeding, applying continuous, heavy pressure for at least 3 minutes. For chest wounds, an occlusive seal must be applied to prevent air from entering the chest cavity and causing lung collapse. Keep the K9 warm and quiet to prevent the onset of shock.

VERBAL COMMANDS: Command onlookers or other deputies: "GET THE K9 TRAUMA KIT NOW!" and "SECURE THE PERIMETER!" By vocalizing these orders, you establish a clear professional record of your immediate and decisive action to save the department's asset.

NARCOTICS INTERDICTION

Scent Pools and Indicators

FIELD TACTICS: Identify "Scent Pools" around vehicle seams. Watch for "Head Snaps" and changes in breathing patterns. A final alert gives you the probable cause required for a search under the Automobile Exception.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "AMHERST COUNTY K9—STAY IN YOUR VEHICLE!" and "K9 ALERT—PROBABLE CAUSE!"
