K9 TACTICAL MEDIC & CPR

K9 CPR: The Number One Priority

PROTOCOL: If the K9 is unresponsive, initiate CPR immediately. Place the K9 on their right side. Compress the chest at 100-120 beats per minute. For Shepherds and Malinois, compress directly over the heart. Give 2 breaths into the nose for every 30 compressions.

VERBAL COMMANDS: Use loud, clear verbalizations for your body cam: "OFFICER DOWN—REQUESTING EMERGENCY VET SECURE TRANSPORT!" and "STAY BACK—MEDICAL EMERGENCY!"

Snake Bites (Pit Vipers)

VA PROTOCOL: Identify if Copperhead or Timber Rattler. Keep the bite area BELOW heart level. DO NOT apply ice or suction. Transport immediately for antivenom. Document the time of the bite for the vet.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "CLEAR THE TRAIL—K9 BITE EXPOSURE!" and "NOTIFY DISPATCH—TRANSPORTING TO EMERGENCY VET!"

NARCOTICS INTERDICTION

The Search Pattern & COB

METHODOLOGY: Start down-wind. Watch for the Change of Behavior (COB): head-snaps or "detailing" seams. A final response (sit/down) establishes probable cause under the Automobile Exception.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "AMHERST COUNTY SHERIFF'S OFFICE K9! REMAIN IN YOUR VEHICLE!" and "K9 ALERT—PROBABLE CAUSE ESTABLISHED!"
