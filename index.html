K9 EMERGENCY - PRIORITY 1

K9 CPR

1. Right side down. 2. Hands behind elbow. 3. 100-120 bpm. 4. 30:2 pump-to-breath ratio (breath into snout).

HEAT STROKE

Cool paws/ears. No ice. Watch for purple gums. Temp > 106° is fatal.

BLOAT (GDV)

Retching, hard belly. Immediate transport. Fatal within 60 mins.

PAW LACERATIONS

Apply pressure. Clean with saline. Wrap tight but check circulation.

GUNSHOT/PENETRATING

Apply direct pressure. Do not remove object. Use chest seal for torso.

HUMAN TACTICAL MEDICAL

TOURNIQUET

High and tight. Twist until bleeding stops. Record time.

NARCAN (OVERDOSE)

Tilt head. Spray full dose into nostril. If no response in 2 min, repeat.

CHEST WOUND

Occlusive dressing taped on 3 sides to prevent tension pneumothorax.

SNAKE BITE (TIMBER RATTLESNAKE)

Keep below heart. Immobilize limb. Do not cut or suck. Transport ASAP.

HYPOTHERMIA/COLD STRESS

Wrap in space blanket. Warm the core. No caffeine/alcohol.

CONSTITUTIONAL CASE LAW

Terry v. Ohio

Stop & Frisk: Requires Reasonable Articulable Suspicion of crime and weapons.

Graham v. Connor

Use of Force: Must be 'Objectively Reasonable' under the circumstances.

Carroll v. US

Vehicle Exception: Probable cause allows search of vehicle without warrant.

Chimel v. California

Search incident to arrest: Limited to the 'lunge area' or immediate control.

Tennessee v. Garner

Deadly Force: Prohibited against fleeing non-dangerous felons.

Mapp v. Ohio

Exclusionary Rule: Illegal evidence is thrown out.
