1. VA CODE § 18.2-144.1

Killing or injuring police animals. Maliciously killing a police animal is a Class 5 felony (up to 10 years). Maliciously wounding with intent to maim is a Class 6 felony. This protection applies during all active LEO assistance.

2. FLORIDA v. HARRIS (SCOTUS)

Established that a K9 alert alone is sufficient for Probable Cause if the dog has bona fide certification. Rejected strict record-keeping requirements for field performance.

3. RODRIGUEZ v. US (SCOTUS)

Police may not extend the duration of a traffic stop "beyond the time reasonably required to complete the mission" to conduct a K9 sniff without separate reasonable suspicion.

4. ILLINOIS v. CABALLES (SCOTUS)

A dog sniff of a vehicle during a lawful traffic stop does not violate the 4th Amendment because it only reveals the presence of contraband, which no one has a right to possess.

5. FLORIDA v. JARDINES (SCOTUS)

Bringing a K9 onto the curtilage (front porch) of a home to sniff for drugs requires a warrant. The porch is a constitutionally protected area.

6. US v. PLACE (SCOTUS)

The "Sniff Test" is not a search under the 4th Amendment when performed on luggage in a public place. It is "sui generis" (unique in its limited disclosure).

7. VA CODE § 18.2-460

Obstruction of Justice. Specifically covers interference with LEO animals. Class 1 misdemeanor to knowingly obstruct a K9 team in the performance of duty.

8. ROBINETTE v. BARNES

Appellate ruling that the use of a properly trained police dog to apprehend a suspect does NOT constitute deadly force.

9. US v. GARCIA

K9 alert on a package in the mail system provides sufficient probable cause for a search warrant.

10. VA CODE § 3.2-6570

Exempts LEO K9 handlers from animal cruelty statutes when the dog is used in the lawful performance of duty (apprehension/containment).

11. CITY OF INDIANAPOLIS v. EDMOND

Checkpoints designed for general "crime control" (drug sniffs of every car) are unconstitutional. Checkpoints must have a specific primary purpose like sobriety or border security.

12. MATTHEWS v. JONES

Standard for deploying K9s on fleeing suspects: Must consider the severity of the crime and the immediate threat to officers or public.

13. US v. SHARP

20-minute detention for a K9 arrival is reasonable if the officer is acting diligently to confirm or dispel suspicion.

14. COMMONWEALTH v. ENOS

VA ruling on the specific cues of a K9 (scratching vs sitting) and how they must be articulated in a search warrant affidavit.

15. US v. LUDWIG

A K9 alert on one part of a vehicle (trunk) provides probable cause to search the entire vehicle, including locked containers.

16. STATE v. MILLER

Evidence from a tracking dog is admissible if the dog's breed is characterized by acute scent, the dog is trained, and the trail is fresh.

17. US v. CEDANO-ARELLANO

Defense has the right to K9 training logs and certification records during discovery to challenge the dog’s reliability.

18. STATE v. WAINMAN

Officer safety allows for the temporary "Terry" frisk of a suspect before or during a K9 deployment if reasonable suspicion of a weapon exists.

19. VA CODE § 19.2-59

Warrant requirements for search and seizure; clarifies that K9 tracking in pursuit of a felon falls under exigent circumstances.

20. US v. THOMAS

A K9 sniff of a hallway outside an apartment door was ruled a search in some jurisdictions—caution is advised without a warrant for multi-unit dwellings.

1. K9 CPR (EMERGENCY)

1. Lay K9 on RIGHT side. 2. Locate heart (where left elbow touches chest). 3. Compressions: 100-120 per minute. 4. 2 Breaths for every 30 compressions. 5. Extend neck to open airway.

2. GUNSHOT / TRAUMA

Pack wound with hemostatic gauze. Apply pressure for 5 mins. For limbs, use K9 Tourniquet high and tight above the wound.

3. SNAKEBITE (Viper)

Immobilize K9. Keep bite BELOW heart. Do not use suction or ice. Rapid transport to Animal ER for antivenin.

4. HEAT STROKE

Temp >106F. Move to shade. Cool paws and ears with water. Do not submerge in ice water (causes shock).

5. NARCAN (OPIOID)

4mg Intranasal. Use for K9 if exposed to Fentanyl. Repeat every 3 mins if breathing is labored.
