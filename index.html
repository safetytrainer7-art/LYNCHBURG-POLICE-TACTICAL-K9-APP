6. HEAT EXHAUSTION & BODY TEMP

Immediately move the K9 to a shaded area or a patrol vehicle with the air conditioning on maximum. Douse the dog’s entire body with cool—not ice cold—water, focusing heavily on the paw pads, groin, and underarms where major blood vessels are closest to the skin. If possible, place the dog in front of a fan to facilitate evaporative cooling, which is the most effective method for lowering a dog's core temperature.

Monitor the dog's temperature every 5 minutes. Stop active cooling once the core temperature reaches 103°F (39.4°C) to prevent rebound hypothermia, which can be just as fatal. Watch for signs of "Heat Stroke," such as dark red or purple gums, thick ropey saliva, or neurological distress. Even if the K9 appears to recover, immediate veterinary evaluation is mandatory to check for delayed organ failure or blood-clotting issues.
