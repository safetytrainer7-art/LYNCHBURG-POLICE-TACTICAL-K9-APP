K9 TACTICAL MEDICAL

K9 HEAT STROKE

Symptoms: Heavy panting, dark red gums, collapse. ACTION: Move to shade. Cool with water on paws/ears. Do NOT use ice water.

K9 PAW LACERATION

ACTION: Apply pressure. Clean with saline. Wrap with Vetrap. Watch for debris/glass in pads.

K9 BLOAT (GDV)

EMERGENCY: Distended abdomen, dry heaving. ACTION: Immediate transport to vet. Fatal if untreated.

K9 GUNSHOT / PENETRATING WOUND

ACTION: Apply pressure. Do not remove objects. Use chest seal for torso wounds.

HUMAN EMERGENCY CARE

1. TOURNIQUET (MASSIVE BLEED)

High and tight. Windlass until bleeding stops. Note time.

2. NARCAN DEPLOYMENT

Tilt head back. Spray full dose into nostril. If no response in 2 mins, repeat.

3. CHEST WOUND (SUCKING)

Apply occlusive dressing. Tape 3 sides to allow air escape.

4. SEVERE HYPOTHERMIA

Remove wet clothes. Warm core first. Do not rub limbs.

5. HEAT EXHAUSTION

Cool environment. Electrolytes. Fan the skin.

6. SNAKE BITE (VA TIMBER RATTLESNAKE)

Keep bite below heart. Do not cut/suck. Rapid transport.

7. FRACTURE / DISLOCATION

Splint as they lie. Check for pulse below injury.

8. ANAPHYLAXIS (STING)

Administer Epi-Pen to outer thigh. Hold for 10 seconds.

9. HEAD TRAUMA (TBI)

Watch for pupils, orientation, vomit. Keep calm.

10. BURNS (THERMAL/CHEMICAL)

Cool water for 20 mins. Cover loosely with sterile dressing.
