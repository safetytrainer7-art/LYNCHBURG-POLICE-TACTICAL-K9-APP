V90 FEDERAL CASE LAW

Florida v. Harris: Established that a K9's alert provides probable cause if the dog is certified or recently trained. Reliability is based on training, not field hits.

Rodriguez v. U.S.: Traffic stops cannot be extended for a K9 sniff without independent suspicion. Sniff must happen during the normal time of the stop.

U.S. v. Place: A K9 sniff of luggage in public is not a "search" under the 4th Amendment.

Illinois v. Caballes: A dog sniff during a lawful traffic stop does not violate the 4th Amendment if it doesn't extend the duration of the stop.

V90 VIRGINIA CASE LAW

VA Standards: Virginia allows warrantless vehicle searches following a positive K9 alert. The alert must be a trained final response (Sit/Stare/Scratch).

Particularized Suspicion: A vehicle alert does not automatically mean you can search the passengers. You must articulate suspicion for the individuals.

Tracking: Admissibility of tracking evidence requires proof of the dog's pedigree and the handler's experience in reading the dog.

V90 K9 FIRST AID

K9 CPR: Heart is behind left elbow. 100-120 compressions per minute. Mouth-to-Snout breaths: 30 compressions to 2 breaths.

Heat Stroke: Signs include deep red gums and thick drool. Cool with water on paws and belly. Stop cooling at 103 degrees.

Bloat (GDV): Hard, swollen stomach and unproductive retching. This is a 10-43 emergency. Immediate vet transport required.

GSW: Apply direct pressure. Use QuikClot if available. Tourniquet limbs high and tight if bleeding is arterial.
