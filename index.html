K9 FIRST AID

TACTICAL MEDICAL PROTOCOLS

BLOAT (GDV): Unproductive retching and a hard belly are 10/10 emergencies. The stomach is twisted. Transport immediately. Do not attempt to feed or water.

NARCAN: Administer 4mg nasally for suspected opioid exposure (lethargy, pinpoint pupils). Large dogs may need repeated doses every 2-3 minutes until responsive.

HEATSTROKE: If temp is over 104°F, use cool water on extremities. NEVER USE ICE WATER—it constricts blood vessels and prevents the core from cooling, causing organ failure.

GUNSHOT/TRAUMA: Apply direct pressure. Use a muzzle if dog is conscious. For heavy limb bleeding, apply a tourniquet high and tight. Keep dog warm to fight shock.

LEGAL BIBLE

FEDERAL STANDARDS

Rodriguez v. US: Traffic stops cannot be extended for a dog sniff without independent Reasonable Suspicion. The dog must be on-scene or RS must be established during the initial mission.

Florida v. Harris: Training records and certifications are the bedrock of Probable Cause. If the dog is certified by a recognized body, the alert stands in court.

VIRGINIA CASE LAW

Florida v. Jardines: You cannot bring a K9 onto the curtilage (porch) of a home without a warrant. Public walkways only.

PC Search: A final alert in VA provides Probable Cause to search the entire vehicle, including all containers, under the Automobile Exception.

DRUGS & SAFETY

IDENTIFICATION & HAZMAT

FENTANYL: Beware of blue "M30" pills. They are lethal synthetics. Use Nitrile gloves and never field test powders. If exposure occurs, use Narcan immediately.

VA WEIGHTS: 10g of Meth or 5g of Cocaine Base triggers mandatory minimums. Document scales and cash to support PWID charges.
