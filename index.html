K9 TACTICAL MEDIC & CPR

K9 CPR: The Number One Priority

If the K9 is unresponsive and you cannot detect a pulse, you must initiate CPR immediately. Position the K9 on their right side on a firm surface. For large working breeds like Shepherds or Malinois, place your hands over the widest part of the rib cage and perform compressions at a rate of 100 to 120 beats per minute, ensuring the chest fully recoils between each pump. Close the K9's mouth and deliver 2 steady breaths into the nostrils for every 30 compressions to maintain oxygenation.

VERBAL COMMANDS: Use high-authority verbalization for scene control and documentation: "OFFICER DOWN—REQUESTING CODE-3 EMERGENCY VET!" and "SECURE THE AREA—MEDICAL IN PROGRESS!" Constant verbal communication ensures that nearby units and dispatch understand the life-threat status of the K9 asset immediately.

Penetrating Trauma & Hemorrhage Control

In the event of a gunshot or puncture, identify both entry and exit wounds. Apply immediate direct pressure using hemostatic gauze. For chest wounds, use an occlusive dressing (chest seal) to prevent a tension pneumothorax, which can lead to rapid lung collapse. Keep the K9 warm to prevent hypothermia, which is a leading cause of death in trauma victims, even in warm weather.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "GET THE K9 TRAUMA KIT!" and "NOTIFY NEAREST EMERGENCY VET—ETA 5 MINUTES!" These commands create a record of your professional duty of care and keep bystanders back while you work to stabilize the dog.

NARCOTICS INTERDICTION

Scent Pools and Scent Cones

Begin your search from the down-wind side of the target vehicle or area. Allow the K9 to work the air naturally to find the "Scent Cone." Watch for a Change of Behavior (COB) such as head-snaps, increased respiration, or intense detailing of door seams or trunk seals. A final alert—typically a sit or down—provides the probable cause necessary for a search under the Automobile Exception.

VERBAL COMMANDS: "AMHERST COUNTY SHERIFF'S OFFICE K9—STAY IN THE VEHICLE!" and "K9 ALERT—PROBABLE CAUSE ESTABLISHED!" This verbal trail is essential for body-worn camera evidence and later court testimony by the Commonwealth Attorney.
