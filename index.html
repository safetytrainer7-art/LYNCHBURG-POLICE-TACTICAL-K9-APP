K9 TACTICAL MEDIC & CPR

Critical Life Support (CPR)

In the event of cardiopulmonary arrest, place the K9 on their right side. For deep-chested breeds (Shepherds/Malinois), compressions must be at the widest part of the chest. Maintain a rate of 100–120 compressions per minute. Use a 30:2 ratio (30 compressions to 2 breaths). Ensure the muzzle is held shut while breathing into the nostrils.

Massive Hemorrhage Control

Apply direct pressure with hemostatic agents (QuikClot) for arterial bleeds. For extremity wounds where pressure fails, utilize a dedicated K9 Tourniquet proximal to the wound. In cases of chest trauma (sucking chest wounds), apply an occlusive dressing immediately to prevent tension pneumothorax.

NARCOTICS INTERDICTION

Scent Cone Identification

Handlers must recognize the "Scent Cone" as it exits vehicle seams or luggage. Note the K9’s Change of Behavior (COB): respiratory changes, tail flagging, and "detailing" of the odor source. A final alert (Passive or Active) must be independent of handler influence to maintain Search Integrity.

The Automobile Exception

Under the Carroll Doctrine, a K9 alert provides the Probable Cause necessary to conduct a warrantless search of a vehicle. Ensure the dog sniffs only the exterior "air space" unless independent suspicion allows for an interior sniff.

LEGAL SHIELD: CASE LAW

Rodriguez v. United States (2015)

The U.S. Supreme Court ruled that a police stop may not be prolonged beyond the time reasonably required to complete the mission of the stop. If the "mission" (ticket writing/license check) is finished, you cannot wait for a K9 arrival without independent Reasonable Suspicion.

Florida v. Harris (2013)

This case established that a K9's reliability is based on their training records. If a K9 has been properly certified and has a consistent training history, their alert is sufficient for Probable Cause, even if no drugs are found (the "Ghost Scent" defense).

Reliability Documentation

In accordance with SWGDOG standards, all training must be documented. Logs must include: Type of Hide, Environmental Distractions, Weather Conditions, and Accuracy Rate. These logs are the primary target of discovery during Suppression Hearings.

Deployment Reports

Every street deployment must be cross-referenced with training data. Discrepancies between training performance and street alerts must be addressed through remedial training and documented to prevent Liability Claims against the Department.

VIRGINIA STATE CODE

VA Code § 3.2-6500

Defines the Duty of Care for all working animals. It provides the legal framework for the protection of K9 assets and the penalties for interfering with or harming a law enforcement work dog during the performance of their duties.

VA Code § 18.2-144.1

Prohibits the killing or injuring of a police animal. This is a Class 5 Felony in the Commonwealth. Handlers should use this code when documenting any assault on their K9 partner during an apprehension or search.
